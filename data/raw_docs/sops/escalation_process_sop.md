# Escalation Process SOP

## Purpose
This SOP defines when and how support requests are escalated to ensure timely resolution, risk mitigation, and stakeholder visibility.

---

## When Escalation Is Required
A support request must be escalated when one or more of the following conditions occur:

- The issue threatens to breach SLA response or resolution targets
- The business impact increases in severity
- The issue cannot be resolved by the assigned support tier
- The issue affects multiple customers or critical systems
- Executive or regulatory visibility is required

---

## Escalation Levels

### Level 1 – Operational Escalation
Triggered when frontline support cannot resolve the issue within expected timeframes.

Actions:
- Assign senior support or technical specialists
- Increase monitoring and update frequency
- Reassess severity and impact

---

### Level 2 – Management Escalation
Triggered when service continuity, customer trust, or SLA compliance is at risk.

Actions:
- Notify support management
- Allocate additional resources
- Prioritize resolution over non-critical work
- Coordinate cross-team dependencies

---

### Level 3 – Executive Escalation
Triggered for critical incidents with significant business, legal, or reputational impact.

Actions:
- Notify executive stakeholders
- Initiate incident command structure if required
- Approve emergency changes or mitigations
- Coordinate external communication if needed

---

## Communication During Escalation
- All updates must be documented in the ticketing system
- Update frequency increases with escalation level
- External communication must be approved by designated authorities

---

## De-escalation
An issue may be de-escalated when:
- The root cause is identified and mitigated
- Service stability is restored
- SLA risk is eliminated

De-escalation decisions must be documented.

---

## Post-Incident Review
For escalated incidents:
- Conduct a root cause analysis
- Document contributing factors
- Record corrective and preventive actions

---

## Review and Changes
This SOP is reviewed periodically and updated as required.
