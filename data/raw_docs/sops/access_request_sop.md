# Access Request SOP (Subject Access Requests)

## Purpose
This SOP defines the procedure for handling requests from individuals seeking access to their personal data.

It applies to all Subject Access Requests (SARs) made under applicable data protection laws.

---

## Request Submission
- Requests must be submitted in writing using an approved request form.
- Requests may be submitted electronically or through approved non-electronic channels.
- Requests made through informal or unsupported channels are not considered valid.

---

## Identity Verification
Before disclosing any information:
- The requester must provide sufficient proof of identity.
- Proof of residence or additional verification may be required.
- Requests submitted by agents require documented authorization from the data subject.

Requests will not proceed until verification is complete.

---

## Acknowledgement
- All valid requests must be acknowledged within **5 working days**.
- Requesters will be informed if additional information is required.

---

## Response Timeline
- Requests must be fulfilled within **30 days** of receiving complete and valid information.
- The response period may be extended by up to **two additional months** for complex or multiple requests.
- Requesters must be informed of any extension within the initial 30-day period.

---

## Data Disclosure
- Individuals are entitled to access personal data where they are the primary subject.
- Data may be withheld if disclosure would adversely affect the rights or freedoms of others.
- Data will be provided in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format where possible.

---

## Fees
- Requests are generally processed at no cost.
- A reasonable fee may be charged for requests that are manifestly unfounded or excessive.

---

## Escalation
Unresolved or disputed requests must be escalated to the Data Protection Officer or designated compliance authority.
