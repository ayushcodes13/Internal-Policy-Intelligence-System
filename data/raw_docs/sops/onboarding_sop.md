# Employee Onboarding SOP

## Purpose
This SOP defines the standard process for onboarding new employees to ensure they are prepared, informed, and authorized to perform their assigned responsibilities.

The objective is to ensure consistent onboarding, appropriate access control, and role readiness.

---

## Scope
This SOP applies to all new employees, contractors, and interns.

No individual may perform independent work until onboarding requirements are completed.

---

## Responsibilities

### Company
The company is responsible for:
- Providing required onboarding materials and training
- Granting system access based on role
- Maintaining onboarding documentation

### Manager
The manager is responsible for:
- Defining role-specific onboarding requirements
- Verifying completion of onboarding tasks
- Ensuring readiness before independent work begins

### Employee
The employee is responsible for:
- Completing required onboarding activities
- Acknowledging policies and procedures
- Complying with company standards

---

## Onboarding Process

### 1. Pre-Access Requirements
Before system access is granted, the employee must:
- Accept company policies
- Complete mandatory compliance or security training
- Submit required documentation

---

### 2. Role Orientation
Employees must receive role-specific orientation, which may include:
- Team responsibilities and workflows
- Internal tools and systems
- Escalation and support processes
- Data handling and security expectations

---

### 3. System Access
Access is granted based on the principle of least privilege.

Access may include:
- Email and communication tools
- Internal knowledge systems
- Operational platforms required for the role

Access must be approved by the manager and relevant system owners.

---

### 4. Training Completion
Required training must be completed within the defined onboarding period.

Training may include:
- Security awareness
- Data protection and privacy
- Operational procedures
- Tool-specific instruction

---

### 5. Onboarding Verification
An onboarding checklist must be completed and verified by the manager.

Completion confirms:
- Training requirements are met
- Access is appropriate
- Employee is authorized to work independently

---

## Documentation
Onboarding records must be retained for audit and compliance purposes.

---

## Review and Updates
This SOP is reviewed periodically and updated as required.
