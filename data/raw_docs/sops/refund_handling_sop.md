# Refund Handling SOP

## Purpose
This SOP defines the internal process for reviewing, approving, and issuing customer refunds in accordance with the Billing and Refund Policy.

The objective is to ensure refunds are processed accurately, consistently, and with proper controls.

---

## Scope
This SOP applies to:
- Subscription refunds
- One-time payment refunds
- Partial or full refunds

This SOP does not define refund eligibility. Eligibility is governed by the Billing and Refund Policy.

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Customer Support
- Receive and log refund requests
- Validate request completeness
- Communicate with customers regarding status

### Finance
- Review refund eligibility
- Verify payment records
- Approve or reject refund requests
- Initiate refund disbursement

### Management
- Review escalated or disputed refunds
- Approve exceptions where applicable

---

## Refund Request Intake
Refund requests may be initiated through approved customer support channels.

Each request must include:
- Customer identifier
- Payment reference
- Reason for refund
- Supporting documentation, if applicable

Incomplete requests must be returned for clarification.

---

## Validation and Review
Finance reviews the request to:
- Confirm eligibility under the Billing and Refund Policy
- Validate payment and transaction details
- Identify prior refunds or disputes
- Determine refund amount (full or partial)

Requests that do not meet eligibility criteria are rejected with documented reasoning.

---

## Approval Workflow
Refund requests progress through the following states:

- **Received**: Request logged and awaiting review
- **Under Review**: Validation and eligibility checks in progress
- **Additional Information Required**: Clarification requested from customer
- **Approved**: Refund authorized
- **Rejected**: Refund denied with justification
- **Completed**: Refund issued and recorded

---

## Refund Execution
Approved refunds are processed using the original payment method unless otherwise specified.

Refund execution includes:
- Initiating payment reversal
- Recording transaction references
- Updating internal financial records

Processing timelines may vary depending on payment providers.

---

## Communication
Customers must be informed when:
- A refund request is received
- Additional information is required
- A refund is approved or rejected
- A refund has been completed

All communication must be documented.

---

## Exceptions and Escalation
Refund requests may be escalated when:
- Policy interpretation is unclear
- The customer disputes a rejection
- High-value refunds are requested
- Legal or regulatory concerns arise

Escalation follows the Escalation Process SOP.

---

## Record Keeping
All refund-related records must be retained for audit and compliance purposes, including:
- Requests
- Decisions
- Approvals
- Transaction confirmations

---

## Review and Updates
This SOP is reviewed periodically and updated as required.
