# Customer Support SOP

## Purpose
This SOP defines the standard process for handling customer inquiries, incidents, service requests, and problems through the support function.

It applies to all supported production services.

---

## Support Channels
All support interactions must be initiated through approved support channels.

Each request is tracked using a centralized ticketing system. Updates and communication occur only within the associated ticket.

---

## Types of Requests

### Inquiry
Requests for information or clarification regarding services, billing, or configurations.

### Incident
An unplanned interruption or degradation of service.

### Service Request
A request for a standard change or operational action.

### Problem
A recurring incident that requires root cause analysis and long-term remediation.

---

## Triage and Authorization
- Only designated customer contacts may raise or approve support requests.
- Requests from non-designated users require approval before processing.
- Approved authorization applies only to the specific request.

---

## Severity Classification
Severity is determined based on business impact and urgency:

- **Severity 1 (Critical)**: Complete service outage in production
- **Severity 2 (High)**: Partial outage or significant degradation
- **Severity 3 (Medium)**: Non-critical service change or issue
- **Severity 4 (Low)**: Informational or low-impact request

Severity may be adjusted during investigation.

---

## Response and Resolution
Response and resolution targets are governed by the Support Service Level Agreement (SLA).

This SOP does not override SLA commitments.

---

## Ticket Handling
- All requests are logged and prioritized based on severity.
- High-severity incidents may trigger immediate escalation.
- Status updates are provided through the ticketing system.
- Root cause analysis may be conducted for critical incidents.

---

## Communication
- All communication must remain within the ticketing system.
- High-severity incidents may require more frequent updates.
- External or customer-facing communications must be coordinated by authorized personnel.

---

## Escalation
Requests may be escalated when:
- SLA targets are at risk
- Business impact increases
- Resolution requires additional authority or resources

Escalated incidents are handled according to escalation procedures.

---

## Responsibilities

### Support Team
- Monitor and respond to tickets
- Classify severity and prioritize work
- Coordinate resolution and escalation
- Document actions and outcomes

### Customers
- Provide accurate and complete request information
- Designate authorized contacts
- Cooperate during investigation and resolution

---

## Review and Changes
This SOP is reviewed periodically and updated as needed.
Changes require appropriate approval.
