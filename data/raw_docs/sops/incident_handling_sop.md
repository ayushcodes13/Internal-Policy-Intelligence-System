# Incident Handling SOP

## Purpose
This SOP defines the standard process for identifying, responding to, containing, and recovering from operational or security incidents.

The objective is to minimise impact, restore services safely, and continuously improve incident response.

---

## Scope
This SOP applies to:
- Security incidents
- Service disruptions
- Data integrity or availability issues

All employees and contractors must report suspected incidents.

---

## Incident Phases

### 1. Identification
Determine:
- What happened
- When it started
- Systems or data affected
- Potential impact

Incidents may be identified through monitoring, user reports, or third parties.

---

### 2. Containment
Actions are taken to limit damage and prevent spread.

Examples:
- Isolating systems
- Disabling access
- Blocking network traffic

Containment actions may be taken before full root cause is known.

---

### 3. Eradication
Remove the cause of the incident.

Examples:
- Removing malicious code
- Fixing vulnerabilities
- Resetting compromised credentials

---

### 4. Recovery
Restore systems and services to normal operation.

Recovery must ensure:
- Security controls are re-enabled
- Data integrity is verified
- Services are stable

---

### 5. Lessons Learned
After resolution:
- Conduct a post-incident review
- Identify root cause
- Document corrective actions
- Update controls or procedures as required

---

## Incident Ownership
Each incident has a designated Incident Manager responsible for:
- Coordinating response activities
- Directing technical teams
- Ensuring documentation
- Managing escalation and de-escalation

---

## Escalation
Incidents must be escalated when:
- Business impact increases
- SLA commitments are at risk
- Multiple systems or customers are affected
- Legal or regulatory exposure exists

Escalation follows the Escalation Process SOP.

---

## Communication
- All actions and decisions must be documented
- External communication requires approval
- Information shared must be accurate and timely

---

## Authority During Incidents
During active incidents, authorized responders may:
- Disable services
- Restrict access
- Apply emergency changes

Such actions must be documented and reviewed post-incident.

---

## Review and Updates
This SOP is reviewed periodically and updated as needed.
