# Data Access Policy

## Scope
This policy defines how access to internal platforms, systems, and data resources is granted, managed, and enforced.

It applies to all employees, contractors, and service accounts.

---

## Access Control Principles

Access to systems and data is governed by the following principles:
- Least privilege: users are granted only the access required to perform their role
- Role-based access: permissions are assigned based on defined roles and responsibilities
- Explicit authorization: access is granted through approved policies only

---

## Access Policy Types

### Platform-Level Access
Platform access controls permissions related to administrative and system-wide functions, including:
- User and group management
- Viewing system analytics
- Managing access policies

Platform-level privileges are restricted to authorized administrators only.

---

### Resource-Level Access
Resource access controls permissions on specific data assets and resources, including:
- Datasets
- Dashboards
- Reports
- Metadata and documentation

Permissions may vary by resource type and ownership.

---

## Actors and Roles

Access may be granted to:
- Individual users
- User groups
- Resource owners (where applicable)

Role assignments determine the scope of permitted actions.

---

## Policy Management

Only users with explicit administrative authorization may:
- Create access policies
- Modify existing policies
- Remove or deactivate policies

Unauthorized changes to access controls are prohibited.

---

## Policy Enforcement

Access policies are enforced across internal systems and APIs where applicable.
Changes to access policies may take time to propagate across systems.

---

## Exceptions

Temporary access exceptions must:
- Be explicitly approved
- Have a defined expiration
- Be reviewed periodically

Undocumented or informal access grants are not permitted.

---

## Compliance and Auditing

All access changes are subject to logging and audit.
Access may be revoked at any time to meet security, compliance, or operational requirements.
