# Incident Response Policy

## Scope
This policy defines the procedures for identifying, responding to, and managing security and operational incidents affecting company systems, data, or services.

It applies to all employees, contractors, and service providers.

---

## Definition of an Incident
An incident includes, but is not limited to:
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Data breaches or data leakage
- Service outages caused by security events
- Malware, phishing, or ransomware activity
- Misuse of access privileges

---

## Incident Reporting
All suspected or confirmed incidents must be reported immediately to the security or operations team.

Employees must not attempt to remediate incidents independently unless explicitly authorized.

---

## Incident Classification
Incidents are classified based on severity:
- Low: minimal impact, no sensitive data involved
- Medium: limited operational or data impact
- High: significant data exposure, service disruption, or legal risk

Severity determines response priority.

---

## Response Process
The incident response process includes:
1. Identification and containment
2. Impact assessment
3. Remediation and recovery
4. Post-incident review

Actions taken must be documented.

---

## Communication
Internal communication regarding incidents is restricted to authorized personnel.

External communication, including notifications to customers or regulators, must be approved by legal and executive leadership.

---

## Data Preservation
Relevant logs, systems, and data must be preserved for investigation and compliance purposes.

---

## Post-Incident Review
A post-incident review must be conducted for medium and high severity incidents to identify root causes and preventive measures.

---

## Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action.

The company reserves the right to take immediate action to contain incidents.
