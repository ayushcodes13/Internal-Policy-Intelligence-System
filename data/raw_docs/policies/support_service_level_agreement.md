# Support Service Level Agreement (SLA)

## Scope
This Service Level Agreement (SLA) defines support availability, response targets, and resolution expectations for internal systems and services.

This SLA applies to all supported production services and end users covered under standard support.

---

## Support Coverage
Support is provided through a centralized service desk acting as the first point of contact for incidents and service requests.

Issues that cannot be resolved at first contact may be escalated to specialized support teams.

---

## Incident Prioritization
Incidents are classified based on business impact and urgency.

### Priority Levels
- **P1 (Critical)**: Severe service disruption affecting core business operations
- **P2 (High)**: Major functionality degraded with significant user impact
- **P3 (Medium)**: Partial functionality issues with moderate impact
- **P4 (Low)**: Minor issues or general requests

---

## Incident Response and Resolution Targets

| Priority | Target Response Time | Target Resolution Time |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| P1     | Immediate            | 2 hours                |
| P2     | 15 minutes           | 8 hours                |
| P3     | 15 minutes           | 24 hours               |
| P4     | 4 business hours     | 3 business days        |

Response and resolution targets are measured monthly and are not guaranteed.

---

## Service Request Targets

| Priority | Target Response Time | Target Resolution Time |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| High   | 1 business hour      | 1 business day         |
| Medium | 4 business hours     | 3 business days        |
| Low    | 8 business hours     | 5 business days        |

---

## Service Desk Performance Targets
The support team aims to meet the following monthly targets:
- 80% of requests responded to within defined response times
- First-contact resolution rate of approximately 65%
- Customer satisfaction target of approximately 85%

These targets are indicative and may vary based on workload and incident volume.

---

## Maintenance and Outages
Planned maintenance may impact service availability and will be communicated when possible.

Emergency maintenance may occur without prior notice in response to critical incidents.

---

## Service Level Breach Handling
In the event of recurring or sustained service level breaches:
- A root cause analysis will be conducted
- Remediation actions may include process or staffing adjustments
- Findings may be reviewed with relevant stakeholders

---

## Out of Scope
This SLA does not cover:
- Unsupported or non-production systems
- Personal or unmanaged devices
- Services explicitly excluded from standard support

---

## Amendments and Termination
This SLA may be amended or terminated with written notice.

Termination of this SLA does not retroactively affect previously provided support.
