# Information Security Policy

## Scope
This policy defines the principles, governance, and responsibilities for protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the company’s information assets.

It applies to all employees, contractors, interns, partners, and third-party service providers who access company systems or data.

---

## Security Objectives
The objectives of this policy are to:
- Protect sensitive and confidential information from unauthorized access or disclosure
- Ensure accuracy, integrity, and reliability of information
- Maintain availability of critical systems and services
- Meet legal, regulatory, and contractual security obligations

---

## Core Security Principles
Information security controls must be designed to ensure:
- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability
- Accountability and auditability
- Proper identification, authentication, and authorization

---

## Governance and Ownership
- The Board and executive leadership are ultimately responsible for information security.
- The security function owns this policy and oversees its implementation.
- Day-to-day enforcement is carried out by IT and security operations teams.

Roles and responsibilities must be clearly defined and segregated to avoid conflicts of interest.

---

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **Security Leadership**: Defines security policies, standards, and risk management practices.
- **Business Owners**: Own information assets and ensure appropriate security controls.
- **IT and Security Operations**: Implement and monitor technical and operational controls.
- **All Users**: Are responsible for safeguarding credentials, data, and systems they access.

---

## Access and Asset Protection
- Information assets must be classified based on sensitivity.
- Access must follow the principle of least privilege.
- Access rights must be reviewed periodically.
- Unauthorized access or misuse of information assets is prohibited.

---

## Incident Management
Security incidents must be reported immediately and handled in accordance with the Incident Response Policy.

Employees must not attempt to resolve incidents independently unless authorized.

---

## Compliance and Auditing
- Compliance with this policy is mandatory.
- Security controls and practices are subject to periodic internal and external audits.
- The organization reserves the right to monitor and audit systems and user activity to ensure compliance.

---

## Exceptions
Exceptions to this policy must be formally approved, documented, and reviewed periodically.

Informal or undocumented exceptions are not permitted.

---

## Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, including suspension or termination of access or employment.
